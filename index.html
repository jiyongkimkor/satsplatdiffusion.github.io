Abstract

Gaussian Splatting has enabled efficient and accurate 3D reconstruction from satellite imagery, but two limitations persist. Geometric accuracy is bounded by the sparse, low-intersection-angle observations typical of satellite acquisitions, and rendering quality degrades in regions with limited view coverage or strong illumination variation across multi-date images.

We present SatSplatDiff, a framework that addresses both limitations within a single pipeline. We introduce a shadow-guided absolute height calibration stage that uses cast shadows as a geometric cue to correct height errors that are otherwise unobservable from near-nadir satellite views alone. We then apply a diffusion-based refinement stage that improves visual fidelity in under-observed regions, using pseudo-real data mixing to keep the generative refinement consistent with the underlying scene geometry.

Across satellite Gaussian Splatting benchmarks, SatSplatDiff improves both geometric accuracy and rendering fidelity over prior splatting-based baselines, while keeping the diffusion-based refinement decoupled from training so it can be applied as a post-processing stage to existing splatting pipelines.
