Abstract

Gaussian Splatting has recently been explored for satellite 3D reconstruction, but the limited top-down viewpoint of satellite imagery leaves building facades under-supervised, producing surface holes and degraded visual fidelity. Generative refinement with pretrained diffusion priors can recover missing appearance detail, but refining each view independently introduces hallucinations and breaks photo-consistency, leading to geometric degradation.

We present SatSplatDiff, which minimizes this geometric degradation. Building on the photogrammetric DSM initialization and 2DGS-based shadow casting from our prior work SatSplat, we first add monocular depth supervision and multi-scale geometric refinement to establish an accurate, well-regularized surface. We then apply shadow-guided generative refinement, where geometrically calculated shadow maps condition the diffusion-based refinement to stay consistent with the underlying geometry, improving visual fidelity while limiting geometric drift.

On the IARPA2016 and DFC2019 datasets, SatSplatDiff reduces geometric MAEreg by up to 18% and improves visual fidelity (FID-CLIP) by 28–45% over existing baselines, delivering up to 5× effective resolution enhancement with minimal hallucination and sensor-consistent appearance. By preserving geometric accuracy throughout, the method also shows seamless cross-tile consistency and strong scalability for large-scale reconstruction.
